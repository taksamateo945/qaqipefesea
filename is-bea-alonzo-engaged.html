Who Is Bea Alonzo?

Born on October 17, 1987, Bea Alonzo is a renowned Filipino actress who holds a special place in the hearts of the Philippine entertainment industry.

Often referred to as the “Queen of Movies,” she has left an indelible mark with her outstanding performances in various blockbuster films.

Some of her most celebrated works include “One More Chance” (2007), “The Mistress” (2012), “Four Sisters and a Wedding” (2013), and “Eerie” (2019).

Bea’s ability to mesmerize audiences has played a significant role in the tremendous success of these movies.

Particularly, “A Second Chance” stands out as the fifth highest-grossing Filipino film in history, earning an impressive ₱556 million at the box office.

Recognized and acclaimed for her talent and on-screen charisma, Bea Alonzo has received numerous accolades in the industry.

As of July 2021, she has amassed over 2.4 billion pesos at the box office since 2010, securing her position as the fourth highest-grossing Filipino actor during that period.

Her achievements are not limited to the silver screen; Bea has also made a notable mark in television.

Fondly known as the “Queen of Philippine Primetime Television,” she has headlined several top-rating series, including “Kay Tagal Kang Hinintay,” “Magkaribal,” “A Love to Last,” and “I Love Betty La Fea.”

In July 2021, Bea Alonzo signed an exclusive contract with GMA Network, further solidifying her status as one of the most sought-after talents in the industry.

Her first show with the network is “Start-Up PH.”

With her undeniable talent, charm, and versatility as an actress, Bea Alonzo continues to captivate audiences, making her one of the most beloved and respected figures in the Philippine entertainment scene.

Her contributions to both cinema and television have cemented her as an iconic figure in Filipino showbiz.

Bea Alonzo Biography

Bea Alonzo, originally named Phylbert Angelli Ranollo Fagestrom, was born on October 17, 1987, in Rizal, Philippines.

Her parents, Philip Fagestrom and Mary Anne Ranollo, gave her a diverse heritage, as her father hailed from Britain.

Despite her parents eventually parting ways, Bea remained close to her family, particularly her younger brother on her mother’s side.

Bea’s educational journey commenced at the Jose Abad Santos Memorial School in Quezon City, where she completed her elementary education in 2001.

Throughout her studies, she attended various schools, including The Fisher Valley College in Taguig, Ususan Elementary School in Ususan, and Colegio de Santa Ana in Taguig.
