Her Mini Bio

Maggie Lawson was born and raised in Louisville, Kentucky.

When she turned eight, she started appearing in local community and dinner theater productions.

By the age of 10, she landed an on-air commercial job at a Louisville TV station.

Which quickly led to a steady six-year role as a TV personality, reporting news specifically aimed at her fellow kids.

When she reached 17, she relocated to Los Angeles to make her debut in professional TV with a recurring part on the sitcom Unhappily Ever After (1995).

She completed her senior year in high school through a correspondence course.

In 2000, she took on twin roles as a model and a nerdy student in the movie Model Behavior (2000).

During her leisure time, she enjoys singing and writing songs.

Career

Maggie Lawson, an actress best known for portraying Detective Juliet O’Hara on the popular show “Psych” from 2006 to 2014, continued to build her career.

Following her time on “Psych,” Lawson took on a dual role in the film “Model Behavior” alongside co-star Justin Timberlake.

She also made appearances in several sitcoms, including “Boy Meets World,” “Home Improvement,” and “Smallville,” among others.

In 2017, 2020, and 2021, Lawson reprised her role as Juliet O’Hara in all three “Psych” movies.

Post-“Psych,” she secured two leading roles in sitcoms: ABC’s “Back in the Game” and CBS’s pilot “Save the Date.

Additionally, she had short stints on shows like “Two and a Half Men,” “The Ranch,” and “The Great Indoors.”

Notably, in 2020, Lawson played the female lead opposite Jason Biggs in the comedy series Outmatched.

What is Maggie Lawson Doing Now?

Following her tenure on “Psych,” the Kentucky native remained committed to television.

She secured a recurring role on “Two and a Half Men” and then went on to appear in “Angel From Hell” and Netflix’s “The Ranch” from 2017 to 2018.

Subsequently, Lawson made appearances on “Lethal Weapon” and “Santa Clarita Diet” before taking on the role of Kay in 2020’s “Outmatched.”
