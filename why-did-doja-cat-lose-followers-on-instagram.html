Doja Cat is facing a backlash from her fans after she posted some tweets that insulted and mocked them.

What did Doja Cat tweet?

Doja Cat, whose real name is Amala Ratna Zandile Dlamini, is known for her quirky and playful personality and her hit songs like “Say So”, “Kiss Me More”, and “Need to Know”.

She has a loyal fan base that calls themselves “kittens”, a reference to her stage name and her love for cats.

However, on July 23, 2023, Doja Cat tweeted some messages that offended and hurt her fans. She wrote:

“I don’t love you guys. I don’t know you.”

“If you call yourself a kitten you need to get off your phone and get a job.”

“My music is a cash grab. I don’t care about any of you.”

“I’m announcing my new album tomorrow. Buy it or die.”

She also posted some screenshots of her Instagram Threads app, where she argued with some fans who tried to defend her.

She called them “annoying” and “delusional” and told them to “shut up”.

Why did Doja Cat tweet those messages?

It is unclear why Doja Cat tweeted those messages, but some fans speculated that she was either joking, trolling, or having a breakdown.

Some also suggested that she was hacked or that someone else was using her phone.

However, Doja Cat did not delete or apologize for her tweets. Instead, she deactivated her Instagram Threads account and posted another tweet that read:

“I’m sorry for being mean to you guys. I love you so much. Please forgive me.”

She also announced her new album, titled “Scarlet”, which is set to be released on October 1, 2023.

She shared the cover art and the tracklist, which features collaborations with artists like The Weeknd, Ariana Grande, Lil Nas X, and SZA.

How did the fans react?

Many fans were hurt, angry, and confused by Doja Cat’s tweets.

They felt betrayed and disrespected by someone they admired and supported.

They expressed their disappointment and frustration on social media, using hashtags like #DojaCatIsOverParty and #DojaCatIsCancelled.

Some fans decided to unfollow Doja Cat on Instagram, causing her follower count to drop from 15.6 million to 15.4 million in one day.

Some also said that they would not buy or stream her new album or attend her upcoming tour.

However, some fans were more forgiving and understanding.

They said that they still loved Doja Cat and that they hoped she was okay.

They said that they would not judge her based on one mistake and that they would support her no matter what.

Doja Cat has not addressed the controversy or explained her tweets since then.
